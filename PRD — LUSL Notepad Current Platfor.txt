## 1. Executive Summary

LUSL Notepad is the official digital learning platform for Limkokwing University Sierra Leone (LUSL).
It provides students with a centralized hub to access course materials, use an AI Study Assistant,

The platform currently serves three types of users: Students, Lecturers, and Administrators,

## 2. Problem Statement

Before LUSL Notepad:
1. Students had no single place to access all course materials across faculties and semesters.
2. AI learning tools (like ChatGPT) were expensive or inaccessible for most students.
3. Lecturers had no structured way to share materials digitally with students.
4. Administrators had no visibility into how content was being used or who was accessing what.

## 10. Security & Access Control

- Role-based routing: users are redirected to the correct dashboard on login
- Content security: all content queries are filtered by faculty + semester (server AND client)
- Students cannot see content from other faculties or semesters
- AI interaction data is scoped by user_id (students only see their own history)
- ContentAccess records are scoped by user_id
- Admin-only backend functions validate the caller's role before executing
- Terms of Service must be accepted before accessing any dashboard feature
- Free query suspension stored on user record (server-authoritative)

## 12. Known Limitations & Issues

1. Student ID prefix (90500) is hardcoded — not configurable per institution
2. Faculty/Program list is hardcoded in multiple files — not database-driven
3. Lecturer access codes are static strings in backend functions — no rotation UI
4. No push notifications — students must check the app for new content
5. No discussion/forum feature — students cannot ask questions publicly
6. PDF viewer relies on browser's built-in viewer — no in-app annotation
7. ElevenLabs podcast only generates single-voice audio (two-voice not yet implemented)
8. No offline mode — requires active internet connection
9. Token purchases are manual (USSD-based) — no auto-confirmation webhook UI
10. No grade tracker — students record quiz scores but not official marks
